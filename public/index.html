About me

I am a data scientist living in Vancouver, Canada. I graduated from Lighthouse Labs' Data Science program and I have a Bachelor of Arts in history from the University of British Columbia. I have a strong background in data science, teamwork, and communication.

Before the COVID-19 pandemic I worked as an airline pilot flying the Saab 340 aircraft, and as a multi-engine and IFR flight instructor for various schools. I have a wide knowledge base and interests, and I have taught classes from meteorology and aerodynamics to air law and crew resource management.

I absolutely love cooking, riding around the city on my bicycle, playing guitar, and reading (sci-fi) books along with a hot cup of coffee.

3380 Books

The end goal of 3380 is to understand the subtext of a book. 3380 uses NLP to look at book reviews; from these, the goal is give the reader a better sense of what to expect from a book.

Outback Predictor

Predicting the sell value of a used Subaru Outback from Craigslist data. Used a Random Forest regressor to find the best price to advertise for any Subaru Outback based on mileage, condition, and type with an accuracy score of 91%.

BR in the BR

An aviation-oriented Discord bot. Its purpose is to collect and parse aviation weather data and present it to my Discord server users. It can decode METAR and TAF records as well as present optional weathercam images.

Vancouver Bike Rides #WIP

Currently working on a project to visualize bicycle ridership in the City of Vancouver. I want to find out when people ride, how they ride, popular routes, how weather affects ridership, and whether the city is on track with its Transportation 2040 plan.

Bicycle data from the City of Vancouver and from Mobibikes.
